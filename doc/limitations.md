## Important Consequence

The current public methods are constructor, inspection, user-action, clock, and mock helpers only.
Anything browser-like beyond that belongs in the next phase, not in ad hoc facade growth.

The raw quote/mod metadata slice (`HTMLQuoteElement.cite`, `HTMLModElement.cite`, and `HTMLModElement.dateTime`) is also implemented, but it only reflects attributes and does not model browser-specific provenance or editing behavior.

`HarnessBuilder.randomSeed(...)` can override the default `Math.random()` and `crypto.randomUUID()` seeds before bootstrap, but both sequences remain deterministic for a given seed.
`window.navigator.mimeTypes`, `window.navigator.languages`, and `window.navigator.plugins` are also deterministic, but intentionally minimal: `mimeTypes` and `plugins` are exposed as `PluginArray`-like surfaces with `length`, `item(index)`, `namedItem(name)`, `keys()`, `values()`, `entries()`, `forEach(callback[, thisArg])`, `refresh()` on `plugins`, and `toString()`, while `languages` is exposed as a `DOMStringList`-like surface with `length`, `item(index)`, `contains(value)`, `keys()`, `values()`, `entries()`, `forEach(callback[, thisArg])`, and `toString()`, and the legacy aliases `userLanguage`, `browserLanguage`, `systemLanguage`, and `oscpu` are fixed to deterministic read-only values.
